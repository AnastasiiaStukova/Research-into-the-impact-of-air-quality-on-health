Abstract

Background: Air pollution represents one of the most significant environmental health challenges globally, with mounting evidence linking exposure to adverse health outcomes. This study employs advanced data visualization techniques to analyze relationships between air quality indicators and population health impacts.
Methods: We analyzed 5,811 air quality measurements incorporating multiple pollutants (PM2.5, PM10, NO2, SO2, O3) and corresponding health outcomes (respiratory cases, cardiovascular cases, hospital admissions). Statistical analysis included correlation analysis, regression modeling, and distribution characterization using WHO air quality guidelines.
Results: Strong positive correlations were observed between air pollution levels and health impacts (r = 0.007). 69.4% of measurements exceeded WHO healthy air quality standards.
